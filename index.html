Introduction

What the page is about

This web page provides tools for people interested in the real estate market in Greater Toronto Area. It displays the condo price trend in the past 20 years and forecasting condo prices for the next four years.

What tools were used to forecast

We used Vector Autoregressive Model (VAR), a statistical time series model, to analyse our data. This model captures the relationship between multiple quantities as they change over time with less demand in time and information. In contrast to the normal ARIMA model which only considers a single variable, it measures different quantities.

What data was used

All the data sources used for analysis were collected from public databases. The datasets for factors potentially influencing condo price, such as GDP, the population in Toronto, were provided by government organizations (e.g., Statistic Canada). Economic indexes like metal price and gold price were found on NGO database (e.g. World Bank). Toronto Reginal Real Estate Board, a non-profit NGO published condo sales and price data for listing regions in Toronto. We used those data for average condo price.

Legals

Many factors influence real estate market, our prediction is not guarenteed to reflect the price in the future. Please use this tool with cautious to decide on your purchase.

Text Summary

This model is for condo price forecasting in the downtown Toronto area. In this model, the price prediction factors included historical condo selling price statistical data and other factors. Considering real estate products are financial derivatives, the economic factors were considered for testing and training this model. Additionally, to explore the influence of the population change on real estate transaction price, related data was quantitatively analyzed in this model. More factors will be concerned and tested in future model updating to approve the accuracy of forecasting results.

Through HTML web pages, we can visually observe and analyze the research results more intuitively. The functions of the website include 1. Interactive Diagrams: query historical or predicted condo prices in the map according to the region; 2. Dashboard: obtain data comparison line graphs through retrieval; 3. Data Table: filter query data in the form of tables.

In data analysis, we used Vector Autoregressive Model (VAR) because our dataset includes yearly, monthly and daily data. In addition, VAR could help to generalize the single-variable autoregressive model by allowing for multivariate time series. Due to the limitation of searching data, the earliest data used in this model is from January 2000. Since the continuity of data time in the dataset is inconsistent, we need to choose a model with less stringent requirements for time and information during analysis. And this model needs to capture the relationship between multiple quantities that change over time and predict serial numbers through time series. VAR satisfies the above requirements well. This prediction model uses Correlation Heatmap for feature engineering. In addition, the month is added as an extracted feature to evaluate sessional influencing factors through industry insights and analysis.

This prediction model uses Correlation Heatmap for feature engineering. In addition, the month is added as an extracted feature to evaluate sessional influencing factors through industry insights and analysis. And through the use of the following analysis methods: PCA Feature Reduction, Granger Causality Test for VAR, Johansen test for VAR, ADFuller test to check for stationarity, Differencing to make dataset stationery, and Durbin–Watson Test.

Model Accuracy Evaluation: More accurate results can be obtained if predict condo price within six months with this model. However, due to the scope of data quantity and frequency, there are errors in the prediction results. In the future, we will look for more data to achieve more accurate condo price forecasts.
